The **Deep Sea Leviathan** is the second boss in Starfall. A massive serpentine creature adapted to the crushing depths of the ocean, it combines devastating charge attacks with clouds of damaging bubbles. Fighting underwater adds an extra layer of difficulty, making preparation essential.

## How to Summon

The Deep Sea Leviathan altar spawns in the **ocean area**, between Y coordinates surface-10 and underground. Look for a blue-tinted altar structure beneath the waves.

Stand near the altar and press **F** with the following ingredients:

| Ingredient   | Quantity |
|--------------|----------|
| Coral        | 15       |
| Sand         | 20       |
| Plant Fiber  | 8        |

All ingredients are consumed when the boss is summoned.

## Phases

### Phase 1 (100%-50% HP)
- **Speed:** 70
- **Damage:** 20
- **Attack Interval:** 2.5s
- The Leviathan swims in wide arcs, periodically charging at the player and releasing bubble projectiles.

### Phase 2 (Below 50% HP)
- **Speed:** 100
- **Damage:** 30
- **Attack Interval:** 1.8s
- The Leviathan becomes far more aggressive. Its charges are faster and it releases bubbles more frequently. The increased speed makes it difficult to outrun.

## Attacks

- **Charge** -- The Leviathan locks onto the player's position and lunges forward at high speed. Telegraphed by a brief pause and slight recoil before the rush.
- **Bubble Barrage** -- Releases a spread of bubble projectiles that drift toward the player. Each bubble deals damage on contact.
- **Tail Sweep** -- At close range, the Leviathan sweeps its tail in an arc, knocking the player back and dealing damage.

## Drops

| Drop              | Details           |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| Thrust Regulator   | Jetpack component (ID 181) |
| XP                 | 150               |

The Thrust Regulator is the second of six jetpack components.

## Strategy

- **Craft a Rebreather first.** This is critical. Without it, you will drown during the fight. Craft one at an Anvil with 30 Coral, 20 Sand, 5 Glass, and 3 Iron Bars.
- **Dodge charges sideways.** The Leviathan charges in a straight line. Move perpendicular to its facing direction to avoid the lunge.
- **Ranged weapons shine here.** Melee is risky underwater because you need to stay close to a fast-moving predator. A bow or staff lets you keep your distance.
- **Watch for bubble walls.** In Phase 2, bubble barrages come frequently. Swim through gaps rather than trying to outrun them.
- **Build a platform.** If you can place blocks to create a small underwater shelter near the altar, you can duck behind it to avoid charges.

## Recommended Gear

- **Weapon:** Iron Sword or Iron Bow (ranged preferred)
- **Armor:** Stone Armor or Iron Armor
- **Consumables:** 10+ Healing Herbs, Rebreather (required)
- **Tip:** Bring extra blocks to build cover if needed. Sand or dirt works fine.

## Lore

Before the crash, long-range scans had flagged this planet's oceans as "low threat." The survey team clearly never dove deep enough. The Deep Sea Leviathan has patrolled these waters for centuries, an apex predator with no natural enemies -- until now. Its bioluminescent scales pulse with an eerie blue glow as it circles its prey, patient and relentless.
