# Potions & Buffs

Potions are consumable items that grant temporary buffs when used. Buffs provide stat bonuses, special abilities, and survival advantages that can make the difference between life and death in tough fights.

## How to Use Potions

1. Place the potion in your **hotbar**.
2. Select the hotbar slot (press **1-9** or scroll wheel).
3. Press **I** to consume the potion.
4. The buff activates immediately. A buff icon appears on the HUD showing the active effect and remaining duration.

Each use consumes one potion from the stack.

## Crafting the Brewing Stand

The **Brewing Stand** is required to craft all potions. Build it at a **Workbench Mk2**:

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Stone | 15 |
| Iron Bar | 3 |
| Glass | 2 |

Place the Brewing Stand in your base and press **C** nearby to see potion recipes.

## Other Healing Consumables

Not all consumables are potions. These basic healing items do not require a Brewing Stand:

| Item | Heal Amount | Stack | Crafted At | Recipe |
|------|------------|-------|------------|--------|
| Healing Herb | 25 HP | 30 | Workbench | 5 Leaves + 2 Plant Fiber |
| Cooked Meat | 50 HP | 20 | Dropped by enemies | N/A (enemy loot) |
| Forcefield Potion | 8s immunity | 10 | Anvil | 3 Iron Bar + 2 Glass + 5 Leaves |

Healing Herb and Cooked Meat are instant heals (press I to use). The Forcefield Potion grants a buff.

## Buff Stacking

- **Different buffs stack.** You can have Ironskin, Rage, Regeneration, and Swiftness all active simultaneously.
- **Same buff does not stack.** Using a second Ironskin Potion while one is active resets the duration rather than doubling the defense bonus.
- There is no limit to how many different buffs can be active at once. For boss fights, stack as many as possible.

## Active Buff Display

Active buffs appear as colored icons on the HUD. Each icon shows:
- The buff type (color-coded)
- Remaining duration (counts down)

When a buff expires, its icon disappears and the stat bonus is removed.

## Recipes Output

All potion recipes produce **3 potions** per craft. This means one set of ingredients gives you 3 uses of the buff.

## Tips

- Brew in bulk. Each recipe makes 3 potions, and ingredients are usually plentiful. Aim for 10+ of each important potion before a boss fight.
- The **Wrath Potion** is the strongest damage buff (+25% all damage) but has the shortest duration (30 seconds) and requires rare materials (Void Crystal). Save it for Phase 3/4 of tough bosses.
- **Ironskin Potion** (+10 defense) is the single most valuable defensive buff. It lasts 60 seconds and the ingredients (2 Iron Bar + 5 Stone) are cheap. Always have these on hand.
- **Forcefield Potion** (8 seconds of total immunity) is your emergency button. Keep one in your hotbar during boss fights for when you are about to die.
- The **Spelunker Potion** trivializes ore hunting. Use it whenever you go underground and you will find significantly more resources.
- **Water Walking Potion** (120 seconds) is essential for the Deep Sea Leviathan fight if you do not want to fight underwater.
- Potions stack to 10 per slot. Keep your most-used potions in dedicated inventory slots so you always know where they are.
