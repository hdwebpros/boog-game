# Difficulty

When starting a new game (or hosting a multiplayer session), you choose one of four difficulty modes. Difficulty affects enemy stats, player survivability, loot rates, and more. Your chosen difficulty is saved with your world and displayed on the pause screen.

## Difficulty Modes

### Easy (Green)
*"Relaxed exploration with forgiving combat."*

A gentler experience for players who want to focus on building, exploring, and enjoying the world without punishing combat.

### Normal (White)
*"The intended Starfall experience."*

Balanced difficulty with standard enemy stats, loot rates, and player HP. This is the default mode.

### Hard (Orange)
*"Less HP, tougher enemies, fewer drops."*

A significant step up in challenge. Enemies hit harder and have more health, while your HP pool is reduced and loot is scarcer.

### Hardcore (Red)
*"Permadeath. One life. No second chances."*

The ultimate challenge. All the stat penalties of Hard mode plus **permadeath** -- when you die, your world is gone.

## Stat Multipliers

Difficulty scales 13+ game parameters. Here are the key multipliers:

| Stat | Easy | Normal | Hard | Hardcore |
|------|------|--------|------|----------|
| Enemy HP | 0.7x | 1.0x | 1.5x | 1.5x |
| Enemy Damage | 0.6x | 1.0x | 1.4x | 1.6x |
| Boss HP | 0.7x | 1.0x | 1.5x | 1.8x |
| Player Max HP | 1.5x | 1.0x | 0.8x | 0.7x |
| Loot Chance | 1.3x | 1.0x | 0.8x | 0.7x |
| I-Frame Duration | 1.4x | 1.0x | 0.7x | 0.5x |

### What This Means in Practice

- **Easy**: You have 150 base HP instead of 100, enemies deal 60% damage, and loot drops 30% more often. Bosses have 30% less health.
- **Normal**: All values at baseline. 100 HP, standard enemy stats, standard loot.
- **Hard**: You have 80 base HP, enemies deal 40% more damage with 50% more health, and loot drops 20% less often. I-frames are 30% shorter.
- **Hardcore**: You have 70 base HP, enemies deal 60% more damage, bosses have 80% more health, and i-frames are halved. Oh, and you only get one life.

## Permadeath (Hardcore Only)

In Hardcore mode, death is permanent. When your HP reaches 0:

- Your save file is deleted
- There is no respawn
- All progress is lost

This applies to all forms of death: enemy damage, fall damage, lava, drowning -- everything.

## Difficulty and Multiplayer

When hosting a multiplayer game, the host selects the difficulty before creating the room. All connected players play under the host's difficulty setting. The difficulty is visible on the pause screen alongside the room code.

## Difficulty and Saves

Your difficulty choice is stored in the save file. When loading a saved game, the difficulty indicator appears next to the save slot name, color-coded to match the mode. You cannot change difficulty on an existing save.

## Tips

- **First playthrough?** Start on Normal. The game is balanced around it.
- **Want a challenge?** Hard mode makes every boss fight a serious test. Stock extra potions and build arenas before fights.
- **Hardcore is for veterans.** Know every boss pattern, carry Forcefield Potions, and never explore without full healing supplies.
- **Easy is not "lesser."** It is a great choice for learning the game, experimenting with builds, or just relaxing.
