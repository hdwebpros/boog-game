# Getting Started

Welcome to **Starfall**. You are a crash-landed astronaut stranded on an alien planet. Your mission: collect 6 jetpack components by defeating powerful bosses, assemble the jetpack, and fly off the planet to escape. This guide covers everything you need to survive your first hours.

## Starting a New Game

When you select **New Game** from the title screen, you first choose a **difficulty mode**:

- **Easy** -- More HP, weaker enemies, more loot. Great for learning.
- **Normal** -- The intended experience. Balanced stats.
- **Hard** -- Less HP, tougher enemies, scarcer loot. A real challenge.
- **Hardcore** -- Permadeath. One life, no respawns. For veterans only.

See the [Difficulty page](/wiki/mechanics/difficulty) for full details on how each mode scales the game.

After choosing difficulty, an **intro cutscene** plays showing your shuttle crashing onto the planet. You can skip it by pressing any key.

## The Story

Your shuttle crash-landed. The planet is hostile, filled with creatures and hazards across diverse biomes. Six bosses guard jetpack components scattered throughout the world. Defeat all six, assemble the jetpack at a Fusion Station, and fly high enough to trigger your escape.

## First Steps

### 1. Punch Trees

Your first priority is gathering **Wood**. Walk up to tree trunks (brown blocks) and hold **LMB (Left Click)** to mine them. You start with no tools, so mining is slow, but wood blocks break with bare hands.

Gather at least **20 Wood** and some **Leaves** to get started.

### 2. Craft a Workbench

Open your inventory by pressing **E**. You can craft a **Workbench** by hand (no station needed) using **10 Wood**. Press **C** to open the crafting menu while near a placed station, but for hand recipes you can craft from anywhere.

Place the Workbench on the ground with **RMB**.

### 3. Craft Basic Gear

Stand near your Workbench and press **C** to open crafting. Make these items first:

| Item | Materials | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Wood Pickaxe | 8 Wood | Mine stone and ores faster |
| Wood Sword | 7 Wood | Defend yourself (8 damage) |
| Wood Bow | 6 Wood + 3 Plant Fiber | Ranged attacks |
| Wood Armor (full set) | 36 Wood total | 5 defense |
| Chest | 15 Wood | Extra storage |
| Healing Herb | 5 Leaves + 2 Plant Fiber | Restores 25 HP |

To get **Plant Fiber**, craft it at the Workbench from 3 Leaves (yields 2 Plant Fiber).

### 4. Build a Shelter

Place blocks with **RMB** to build walls and a roof. Enemies spawn at night (Vampires, aggressive Gloom Moths), so having a shelter keeps you safe while you plan your next move.

### 5. Go Underground

Once you have a Stone Pickaxe or better, start digging down. The world has distinct depth layers:

| Layer | Depth (tiles) | What You Find |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Surface | 0-100 | Trees, grass, sand, surface enemies |
| Underground | 100-180 | Iron ore, crystal caverns, cave bats |
| Deep Underground | 180-640 | Diamond ore, titanium ore, lava serpents |
| Core | 640-1320 | Carbon fiber, obsidian, corrupted drones |
| Deep Core | 1320-1600 | Rare materials, extreme danger |

Bring **Torches** (craft 4 from 1 Wood + 1 Plant Fiber) to light your way. Place them with RMB.

## Inventory Management

### Hotbar

The bottom of the screen shows your **10 hotbar slots**. These are your quick-access items. Press **1-9** or use the **scroll wheel** to select a slot. The selected slot determines what you mine with, attack with, place, or use.

### Full Inventory

Press **E** to open the full inventory panel. This shows:

- **Item grid**: All your carried items
- **4 Armor slots**: Helmet, Chestplate, Leggings, Boots (right side)
- **3 Accessory slots**: Below the armor slots

Click items to move them between slots. Click an armor piece, then click the appropriate armor slot to equip it.

### Stacking Rules

| Item Type | Stack Limit |
|-----------|-------------|
| Blocks (dirt, stone, wood, etc.) | 99 |
| Potions | 10 |
| Tools, weapons, armor | 1 (no stacking) |
| Silver Coins | 999 |
| Healing Herb | 30 |
| Cooked Meat | 20 |

### Chests

Craft a **Chest** at a Workbench (15 Wood). Place it with RMB and click it to open. Chests provide extra storage slots so you can stockpile materials at your base.

### Dropping Items

Press **Q** to drop the currently selected item. Dropped items appear on the ground and will despawn after a while. There is no auto-delete system -- you manually manage what you keep and what you discard.

## Crafting Progression

The crafting chain determines your overall progression through the game:

```
Hand Crafting (no station)
  -> Workbench (10 Wood)
    -> Workbench Mk2 (15 Wood + 10 Stone)
      -> Furnace (20 Stone, at Workbench Mk2)
        -> Anvil (10 Iron Bar, at Furnace)
          -> Tech Bench (8 Diamond + 5 Iron Bar + 3 Glass, at Anvil)
            -> Fusion Station (10 Titanium Bar + 5 Diamond + 5 Glass, at Tech Bench)
```

Additional specialized stations branch off this chain:

- **Brewing Stand** (15 Stone + 3 Iron Bar + 2 Glass, at Workbench Mk2) -- brew potions
- **Arcane Anvil** (8 Iron Bar + 4 Diamond + 5 Arcane Dust, at Anvil) -- create enchantments
- **Void Forge** (20 Brimstone + 10 Hellfire Core, at Fusion Station) -- endgame void gear

## Material Tiers

Your gear follows a progression of 6 material tiers:

| Tier | Material | Where to Find | Station Needed |
|------|----------|---------------|----------------|
| 0 | Wood | Surface trees | Workbench |
| 1 | Stone | Everywhere underground | Workbench Mk2 |
| 2 | Iron | Underground layer | Anvil (smelt at Furnace) |
| 3 | Diamond | Deep underground | Tech Bench (smelt at Furnace) |
| 4 | Titanium | Deep underground / Core | Fusion Station (smelt at Tech Bench) |
| 5 | Carbon Fiber | Core layer | Fusion Station |

## Early Game Goals Checklist

1. Gather 20+ Wood and craft a Workbench
2. Craft Wood Pickaxe, Wood Sword, and Wood Armor
3. Craft Torches and a Chest
4. Build a small shelter near spawn
5. Upgrade to Workbench Mk2 and craft Stone tools/armor
6. Mine iron ore underground and smelt Iron Bars at a Furnace
7. Craft an Anvil and make Iron gear
8. Explore surface biomes (8 types: Plains, Forest, Desert, Mountains, Lake, Snow, Jungle, Mushroom)
9. Start collecting boss summoning materials for the Vine Guardian

## Day/Night Cycle

The world has a day/night cycle. During the day, most surface enemies are passive or absent. At night:

- **Vampires** spawn on the surface and are aggressive
- **Gloom Moths** become aggressive (passive during day)
- Visibility decreases (craft Night Owl Potions or get the Miner's Lantern accessory)

Stay near your shelter at night until you have strong enough gear to fight comfortably.

## Death and Respawning

When you die, you **respawn at your spawn point** and **keep all your items**. There is no item loss on death, so don't panic. However, you will need to travel back to where you died if you were exploring far from base.

**Exception: Hardcore mode.** In Hardcore difficulty, death is permanent. Your save file is deleted and all progress is lost. See [Difficulty](/wiki/mechanics/difficulty) for details.
