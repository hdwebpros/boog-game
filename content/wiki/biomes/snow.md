The Snow biome is a frigid expanse of white. Snow blankets the ground, ice formations jut from the terrain, and the air seems to shimmer with cold. Two aggressive predators call this biome home -- the swift Frost Wolf and the ethereal Ice Wisp. The resources here are unique to the frozen landscape, and the challenging enemies make this a mid-game destination for most players.

## Terrain & Generation

The Snow biome generates with snow-covered terrain over frozen stone and ice. The surface is relatively flat compared to Mountains but has gentle rolling hills. Ice blocks form in clusters, sometimes creating slippery patches and frozen pillars. The underground beneath the Snow biome transitions from frozen stone to regular stone as you dig deeper.

## Enemies

- [Frost Wolf](/wiki/enemies/frost-wolf) -- A swift predator that charges through the frozen tundra. Fast movement speed and aggressive pursuit AI make it dangerous.
- [Ice Wisp](/wiki/enemies/ice-wisp) -- A floating spirit of cold that swoops at travelers. Its aerial movement pattern makes it hard to pin down with melee.

Both enemies in the Snow biome are notably more aggressive than Plains or Forest threats. Coming here with at least iron-tier gear is recommended.

## Tips

- Frost Wolves are fast. Do not try to outrun them -- stand and fight with a decent weapon.
- Ice Wisps swoop in predictable arcs. Time your attacks for when they reach the bottom of their arc.
- The Snow biome is a good mid-game challenge. Come here after you have iron or diamond gear.
- Ice blocks can be used as building materials for a distinctive base aesthetic.

## Notable Structures

The Snow biome does not contain special structures. Occasional ice spire formations rise above the terrain, creating natural landmarks that help with navigation.
