The Desert is a vast expanse of sand and sandstone baking under an unforgiving sky. Cacti dot the landscape, and the terrain rolls in dune-like formations. Unlike the gentle Plains, the Desert hosts aggressive enemies that can catch unprepared players off guard. The resources here are unique though -- sand can be smelted into glass, and sandstone is a sturdy building material.

## Terrain & Generation

The Desert generates with undulating sandy terrain. Sand blocks make up the surface layer with sandstone beneath. The lack of trees means wide open sightlines, but also no natural shelter. Cacti grow in scattered clusters and can be harvested. The terrain tends to have more elevation variation than the Plains, with dune-like ridges and shallow valleys.

## Enemies

- [Sand Scorpion](/wiki/enemies/sand-scorpion) -- A burrowing arachnid that charges from the sands. Fast and aggressive, it can close distance quickly.
- [Dust Devil](/wiki/enemies/dust-devil) -- A whirling sand spirit that attacks from range with projectiles. Harder to deal with if you lack ranged weapons.

The Desert is notably more dangerous than the Plains or Forest. Both enemies here are aggressive and will pursue you. The open terrain means there is nowhere to hide.

## Resources

| Resource | Source | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Sand | Ground blocks | Smelt into Glass at a furnace |
| Sandstone | Below sand layer | Sturdy building material |
| Cactus | Cactus plants | Harvested directly |
| Glass | Smelted sand | Used in crafting and building |

## Tips

- Do not venture into the Desert without at least a stone-tier weapon. Sand Scorpions hit hard and are much faster than Space Slugs.
- Bring blocks from another biome to build quick shelters against Dust Devils. The Desert has no natural cover.
- Sand is one of the few resources you can smelt into Glass, making the Desert worth visiting even if you do not plan to stay.
- Dust Devils attack from range. Close the gap quickly with a melee weapon, or fight fire with fire using a bow.

## Notable Structures

The Desert does not contain permanent structures, but its distinct sandstone underground layer can reveal cave openings more easily than grass biomes due to the lighter-colored terrain.
