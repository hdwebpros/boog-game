The Forest biome is a thick canopy of trees stretching across the surface. If the Plains is where you get your bearings, the Forest is where you stock up. Wood is the backbone of early-game crafting, and no biome provides it more generously than this one. The dense tree cover can make navigation tricky and obscure approaching enemies, but the threats here remain mild.

## Terrain & Generation

The Forest generates with gently rolling terrain covered in closely packed trees. The canopy of leaves can block your view of the sky, and the density of trunks creates natural corridors. The ground is grass and dirt, similar to the Plains, but with far more trees per chunk. Occasional clearings break up the woodland.

## Enemies

- [Space Slug](/wiki/enemies/space-slug) -- The same sluggish threat found in the Plains. Their patrol AI means they wander between trees without actively pursuing you.

Enemy density in the Forest is comparable to the Plains. The main hazard is losing sight of a Space Slug behind a tree trunk and walking into it while focused on chopping wood.

## Tips

- If you need large quantities of wood, head straight for the Forest. You can fill your inventory in minutes.
- The dense tree cover provides natural shelter from flying enemies that might wander in from adjacent biomes.
- Clear-cutting a section of Forest creates a decent building site, and you get a mountain of wood in the process.
- Watch your footing. The tree density can make it hard to see gaps or drops in the terrain.

## Notable Structures

The Forest biome does not contain special structures. Its primary purpose is as the game's premier wood-farming location.
