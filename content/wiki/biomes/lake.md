The Lake biome (also referred to as the Ocean) is a large body of water at surface level. Beneath its surface lie coral reefs, sandy floors, and aquatic creatures both passive and predatory. The water itself is both a resource and a hazard -- extended time underwater will drown you without proper preparation. The unique materials found here, particularly coral, cannot be obtained anywhere else.

## Terrain & Generation

The Lake generates as a depression in the surface terrain filled with water tiles. The lakebed is composed of sand with coral formations growing from it. The water can be quite deep, with some lake biomes extending well below the surface level. Seaweed grows in clusters along the bottom. The shoreline transitions gradually from the adjacent biome into shallow water.

## Enemies

- [Fish](/wiki/enemies/fish) -- Passive aquatic creatures that swim around without attacking. They drop minor resources.
- [Anglerfish](/wiki/enemies/anglerfish) -- A deep-water predator that lures prey with its bioluminescent light. Deals heavy damage and lurks in the deepest parts of the lake.

The shallow areas are safe, populated only by passive Fish. The deeper you go, the more likely you are to encounter Anglerfish, which are significantly more dangerous.

## Tips

- Watch your breath. Without a Rebreather or Featherfall Potion, extended deep diving is risky.
- Anglerfish are deadly in the water where your movement is restricted. Lure them to shallower areas if possible.
- Coral is exclusive to this biome, so gather it when you find a Lake.
- Building a bridge across a large lake is often easier than swimming through it, especially early game.
- The Lake biome's sand is an alternative source if you have not found a Desert.

## Notable Structures

Coral reef formations generate naturally on the lakebed, creating colorful underwater landscapes. These are the only source of coral blocks in the game.
