The Mountains biome rises sharply above the surrounding terrain, its craggy peaks and steep cliffs composed of exposed stone. For miners, this is an early goldmine -- iron ore veins are visible right on the surface, saving you the trouble of digging deep. The tradeoff is the Mountain Hawk, the fastest flying enemy on the surface, which dives from the peaks with alarming speed.

## Terrain & Generation

The Mountains generate with dramatically elevated terrain, reaching well above the standard surface level. The ground is primarily stone blocks with dirt only in shallow pockets. The steep inclines and cliff faces create challenging navigation, with drops that can deal fall damage. Iron ore deposits are exposed on cliff faces and mountain surfaces, making them accessible without deep mining.

## Enemies

- [Mountain Hawk](/wiki/enemies/mountain-hawk) -- The fastest flying enemy on the surface. It dives from above with a swooping attack pattern, dealing significant damage on contact.

Mountain Hawks are the primary danger here. Their speed and aerial approach make them hard to hit with melee weapons. A ranged weapon or good timing is essential.

## Tips

- Visit the Mountains early if you want to skip straight to iron-tier equipment. The exposed ore saves significant digging time.
- Bring a ranged weapon to deal with Mountain Hawks. Their dive pattern is predictable but fast -- dodge sideways and shoot.
- Be careful with fall damage. The steep terrain can lead to long drops if you are not watching your footing.
- The elevated terrain makes the Mountains a natural vantage point for scouting the surrounding biomes.

## Notable Structures

Mountain peaks occasionally form natural overhangs and shallow caves where ore deposits cluster. These are not generated structures per se, but the terrain generation creates useful mining alcoves.
