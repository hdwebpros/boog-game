The Core is the deepest layer of the overworld, beginning at y=1320 and extending to the bottom of the map. This is the planet's molten heart -- a hellscape of heavy lava, carbon fiber deposits, and relentless Corrupted Drones. Carbon fiber is the highest-tier overworld material, and this is the only place to find it. The Core is not a place to explore casually. Come prepared with the best gear you have, or do not come at all.

## Terrain & Generation

The Core generates as a dense, claustrophobic network of tunnels and small chambers. Lava is far more prevalent here than in the Deep Underground, with some areas being more lava than stone. Carbon fiber deposits appear as distinctive veins in the walls, standing out from the surrounding dark stone. The terrain is tight and treacherous, with fewer open caverns and more narrow passages flanked by lava.

## Enemies

- [Corrupted Drone](/wiki/enemies/corrupted-drone) -- A malfunctioning machine that fires projectiles from a distance. These are the only enemies in the Core, but they are persistent and dangerous.

Corrupted Drones attack at range, which is especially dangerous in the Core's tight tunnels where dodging is limited. Their projectiles hit hard, and they can fire from positions you cannot easily reach.

## Tips

- Carbon fiber is the reason you are here. It is the top-tier material for the overworld, needed for the best pre-void equipment.
- Corrupted Drones fire projectiles. Bring a shield or be ready to dodge in narrow corridors.
- The lava density in the Core is extreme. Carry plenty of blocks for bridging and always watch where you step.
- Titanium-tier gear is the minimum recommended equipment for the Core. Diamond gear will work but you will take significant damage.
- Mine methodically. Carbon fiber veins are rare, and you do not want to miss any in the dangerous environment.
- Consider bringing healing items in bulk. The constant Corrupted Drone attacks and lava hazards will drain your health steadily.

## Notable Structures

The Core does not contain distinct structures. The entire layer is a hostile environment defined by its extreme lava density and carbon fiber deposits. The narrow passages and lava channels create a natural maze that can be difficult to navigate without leaving markers.
