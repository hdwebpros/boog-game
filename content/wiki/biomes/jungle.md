The Jungle is a suffocating tangle of vegetation, vines, and oversized flora. Every surface is covered in green, and the canopy overhead is so thick it almost blocks out the sky. The Jungle's enemies are ambush predators -- the Jungle Spider drops from above while the Vine Strangler patrols the undergrowth. This biome rewards careful, observant players with abundant organic resources.

## Terrain & Generation

The Jungle generates with dense tree coverage even thicker than the Forest biome. Jungle grass covers the ground, and vines hang from elevated terrain and tree branches. The terrain has moderate elevation variation with natural platforms created by thick vegetation. The undergrowth is so dense that visibility is limited in all directions.

## Enemies

- [Jungle Spider](/wiki/enemies/jungle-spider) -- A camouflaged predator that ambushes from the canopy. It drops down onto unsuspecting players passing below.
- [Vine Strangler](/wiki/enemies/vine-strangler) -- A thick, plant-like creature that patrols the jungle floor. Slow but tough, it blends into the surrounding vegetation.

The Jungle's enemies rely on surprise. The Spider's ambush tactic is particularly effective in the dense canopy where you cannot easily see above. The Vine Strangler's green coloring makes it hard to spot among the jungle grass.

## Tips

- Look up frequently. Jungle Spiders ambush from above, and spotting them before they drop gives you a huge advantage.
- Vine Stranglers blend into the foliage. Move carefully and watch for movement in the undergrowth.
- Vines are only found here, so collect them if you need them for crafting.
- The dense canopy provides natural shelter from flying enemies, similar to the Forest but even more effective.
- Consider bringing torches or a light source. The thick canopy can make the Jungle feel dark even during the day.

## Notable Structures

The Jungle does not contain specific structures, but its dense vegetation creates natural chambers and enclosed areas that feel almost cave-like on the surface. These clearings can serve as natural building sites.
