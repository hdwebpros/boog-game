The Mushroom biome is one of the strangest places on the surface. Giant mushroom stalks rise from the ground like trees, their caps forming a colorful canopy overhead. The terrain itself is composed of spongy mushroom blocks, and the air seems thick with spores. Tucked between other biomes, the Mushroom biome is small but distinctive, offering unique building materials and a taste of the fungal creatures you will encounter underground.

## Terrain & Generation

The Mushroom biome generates as a narrow zone between other surface biomes. The terrain is composed of mushroom blocks in various colors, with giant mushroom structures replacing trees. The ground has a soft, uneven quality to it with gentle mounds. The biome is typically smaller than other surface biomes, making it easy to pass through quickly.

## Enemies

- [Fungal Shambler](/wiki/enemies/fungal-shambler) -- A mushroom creature found primarily underground, but it can also appear in the surface Mushroom biome. Splits into Sporelings on death.
- [Sporeling](/wiki/enemies/sporeling) -- Tiny spore creatures spawned by a dying Fungal Shambler. Weak individually but appear in numbers.

The Mushroom biome shares its enemies with the Underground. Fungal Shamblers are tougher than typical surface enemies, so be prepared.

## Tips

- Mushroom blocks are exclusive to this biome and make for distinctive building materials if you want a unique base aesthetic.
- When you kill a Fungal Shambler, be ready for the Sporelings that spawn from it. They are weak but can overwhelm you if you are not paying attention.
- The Mushroom biome is small. If you need mushroom blocks, gather them efficiently because you may not find another one nearby.
- This biome serves as a preview of the fungal enemies you will face underground. Use it as practice.

## Notable Structures

Giant mushroom formations serve as the biome's equivalent of trees. Their thick stalks and wide caps create natural sheltered areas beneath them.
