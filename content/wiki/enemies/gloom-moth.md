The Gloom Moth is a flying insect found from the surface down to the deep underground. During daylight hours it is completely passive, fluttering harmlessly. However, once night falls, the Gloom Moth becomes aggressive, swooping at players with surprising ferocity. This dual-nature makes it an unpredictable enemy that can catch players off guard during the day-to-night transition.

## Behavior

The Gloom Moth uses a **swoop** AI pattern, but only at night. During the day, it flies passively and does not attack. When night falls, it switches to aggressive swooping behavior, diving at the player from above. The transition happens immediately when the day/night cycle changes, so moths that seemed harmless moments ago can suddenly begin attacking.

## Location

- **Layers:** Surface, Underground, Deep Underground
- **Biomes:** All
- **Spawn conditions:** Present day and night; passive during day, aggressive at night

Gloom Moths have an unusually wide layer range, appearing from the surface all the way to the deep underground. Underground, where there is no day/night cycle, their behavior depends on the surface time.

## Strategy

During the day, Gloom Moths are free XP -- walk up and hit them without risk. At night, treat them like faster Cave Bats. Their swoop pattern is similar but their higher speed makes timing dodges more difficult. If you are exploring underground and lose track of the surface time, the sudden aggression of nearby Gloom Moths serves as a useful indicator that night has fallen. In mixed combat situations at night, prioritize more dangerous enemies and deal with moths between attacks.

## Special Traits

- **Day/Night Behavior Switch:** The Gloom Moth is passive and harmless during the day but becomes an aggressive swooping attacker at night. This is unique among enemies in the game.
