The Shock Beetle is an armored insect found in the underground and deep underground layers. It charges at players like a Rock Golem but adds a dangerous twist: when struck, it retaliates by firing an electric projectile. This retaliation mechanic punishes reckless attacking and forces players to think about positioning with every swing.

## Behavior

The Shock Beetle uses a **charge** AI pattern. It walks along the ground and charges toward the player when in range, dealing contact damage. What sets it apart is its retaliation mechanic -- every time the Shock Beetle takes damage, it fires a projectile back at the attacker. This creates a risk-reward dynamic where each hit you land also puts you in danger.

## Location

- **Layers:** Underground, Deep Underground
- **Biomes:** All (underground)
- **Spawn conditions:** Any time

Shock Beetles are moderately common throughout the underground layers and become slightly more frequent in the deep underground.

## Strategy

The key to fighting Shock Beetles is dodging the retaliation projectile after every hit. Hit once, dodge sideways, then re-engage. Do not spam attacks, as the rapid-fire retaliation projectiles will out-damage you. Ranged weapons are excellent because you can fire from a distance and have more time to dodge the return projectile. High-damage weapons that kill the beetle in fewer hits mean fewer retaliation shots to deal with. The 40% knockback resistance means you cannot easily push it away, so focus on lateral movement rather than trying to create distance.

## Special Traits

- **Retaliation Projectile:** When hit by any attack, the Shock Beetle fires an electric projectile back at the player. This makes every offensive action a calculated risk.
- **40% Knockback Resistance:** The Shock Beetle resists a moderate amount of knockback, making it harder to keep at range with melee weapons.
