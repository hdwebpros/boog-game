The Anglerfish is an ocean-dwelling predator that uses its bioluminescent lure to draw in curious players before striking. Found exclusively in ocean biome waters, it is one of the more dangerous aquatic threats. Its moderate HP and damage make it a real danger for players exploring underwater, where movement is already restricted.

## Behavior

The Anglerfish uses a **lure** AI pattern. It floats relatively still in the water, appearing passive at first glance. When a player approaches within range, attracted by the glowing lure, the Anglerfish lunges forward with surprising speed to bite. After lunging, it retreats briefly before resetting.

## Location

- **Layers:** Ocean
- **Biomes:** Ocean / Lake (underwater areas)
- **Spawn conditions:** Ocean tiles only

Anglerfish spawn exclusively in ocean water. You will only encounter them when diving beneath the surface of lakes and ocean areas.

## Strategy

Fighting underwater is inherently difficult due to reduced movement speed and limited escape routes. The key to fighting Anglerfish is recognizing the lure before you swim into range. Approach cautiously and attack from above or the side rather than head-on. Ranged weapons are extremely effective since you can shoot from outside lunge range. If you must melee, strike quickly and retreat before the lunge connects. The Coral drops are useful for crafting, making Anglerfish worth hunting once you are comfortable with underwater combat.

## Special Traits

The Anglerfish has no special traits beyond its lure behavior, but the aquatic-only environment it inhabits creates a natural challenge due to restricted player movement underwater.
