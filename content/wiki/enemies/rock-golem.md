The Rock Golem is a heavily armored stone creature that roams the underground and core layers. With 80 HP and significant knockback resistance, it is one of the toughest non-boss enemies in the upper world. Its charge attack deals heavy damage and can catch unprepared players off guard, making it a genuine threat during underground exploration.

## Behavior

The Rock Golem uses a **charge** AI pattern. It walks slowly back and forth until it detects the player within range. Once triggered, it accelerates into a rushing charge directly toward the player, dealing heavy contact damage. After charging, it briefly pauses before resetting and potentially charging again.

## Location

- **Layers:** Underground, Deep Underground, Core
- **Biomes:** All (underground)
- **Spawn conditions:** Any time

Rock Golems are found throughout the underground layers and become more common near the core. Their wide layer range means you will encounter them frequently during extended mining sessions.

## Strategy

The Rock Golem's charge is powerful but predictable. When you see it begin to charge, jump over it or move to a higher platform. Its 60% knockback resistance means you cannot push it back easily, so stand-and-trade tactics are inadvisable. Ranged weapons are the safest approach, allowing you to chip away at its high HP from a distance. If using melee, hit-and-run tactics work best: land a blow, dodge the charge, then re-engage. The Iron Ore drops make Rock Golems a worthwhile farming target once you can handle them comfortably.

## Special Traits

- **60% Knockback Resistance:** The Rock Golem resists most knockback effects, making it difficult to keep at a distance with melee attacks.
