The Fungal Shambler is a mushroom-like creature that patrols the underground and deep underground layers. While slow and relatively weak on its own, it has a dangerous surprise: upon death, it splits into three fast-moving Sporelings. This makes the Fungal Shambler deceptively threatening, as killing it creates an immediate secondary fight that can catch players off guard.

## Behavior

The Fungal Shambler uses a **patrol** AI pattern. It moves slowly back and forth along the ground, reversing at walls and ledges. It does not pursue the player and only deals damage on contact. Its real threat comes from its death effect -- when killed, it immediately spawns three Sporelings at its location.

## Location

- **Layers:** Underground, Deep Underground
- **Biomes:** All (underground), especially common in Mushroom biome underground
- **Spawn conditions:** Any time

Fungal Shamblers are moderately common throughout the underground layers. They tend to appear in clusters, which can lead to swarms of Sporelings if multiple Shamblers are killed in quick succession.

## Strategy

Always be prepared for the Sporeling phase. Kill the Shambler with a ranged weapon if possible, giving you distance from the Sporelings that spawn. If using melee, swing immediately after the killing blow to catch the Sporelings before they scatter. Avoid killing multiple Shamblers at once in tight spaces, as the resulting swarm of Sporelings can overwhelm you. The Sporelings despawn after 6 seconds, so retreating briefly is also a valid strategy.

## Special Traits

- **Splits on Death:** When killed, the Fungal Shambler splits into 3 Sporelings that are fast (speed 90) but short-lived (6-second despawn timer). This is the defining trait of this enemy and makes every kill a two-phase encounter.
